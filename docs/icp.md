# Telos AI — Ideal Customer Profile

**Core positioning: "Customising Technology for Your Business."**
Internal working doc — not published on the site. Update it as real client

## 1. Who the ideal customer is

Not "small businesses". The bullseye is:

**An owner-operated local service business, 1–15 people, revenue roughly
£100k–£1m, in Bristol or the wider UK, that wins work through reputation and
enquiries — and whose web presence is visibly worse than the quality of their
actual work.**

Concretely, the kinds of businesses the site and demos are already built
around:

- **Trades and home services** — plumbers, electricians, roofers, landscapers,
  cleaning companies. High enquiry value, phone-led, terrible at follow-up
  because they're on the tools all day.
- **Clinics and practices** — physio, dental, aesthetics, chiropractic.
  Bookings are the whole business; every missed call is a £50–£300
  appointment gone.
- **Salons, barbers, and beauty studios** — busy chairs, quiet inbox
  management, no-shows and rebooking handled by hand.
- **Fitness studios and coaches** — gyms, PTs, yoga/pilates studios. Lead
  flow from Instagram that dies in the DMs.
- **Independent hospitality with bookings** — the pizzeria/café/salon demo
  set exists precisely because these owners *see themselves* in it.

**The tell-tale profile marker:** the business is good at what it does and
busy enough to miss calls — but its website is a 2015 template, a
Facebook page, or nothing. They know it's costing them; they've just never
met a supplier who didn't feel like an agency invoice with a logo.

**Who signs the cheque:** the owner. There is no committee. One conversation,
one decision-maker, decisions made on trust and evidence, not RFPs.

## 2. Their pain points

In their words, not ours:

1. **"I miss calls when I'm working, and they just ring the next guy."**
   Missed calls and slow replies are the #1 leak. Every missed enquiry also
   costs the referrals that client would have generated.
2. **"I do my admin at 9pm."** Quoting, chasing, rebooking, reminders —
   skilled hours spent on work a system should do for pennies.
3. **"My website embarrasses me."** It doesn't reflect the quality of the
   work, doesn't convert, and they don't know who to trust to fix it.
4. **"I paid an agency once. Never again."** Burned by retainers with
   nothing to show, jargon, and tools they were left to operate alone.
5. **"Leads go cold because I don't follow up."** No pipeline, no CRM, or a
   CRM they signed up for and never opened.
6. **"I can't hire a receptionist for £100 a month."** They know the fix is
   a person answering — they just can't justify the salary.

## 3. What Telos actually solves for them

Map each pain to a product we already ship:

| Their pain | What we install | Outcome they feel |
|---|---|---|
| Missed calls | **AI Receptionist + Missed-Call Recovery** — every unanswered call gets an instant, intelligent SMS; calls answered and qualified 24/7 | "I stopped losing jobs I never knew I'd lost" |
| Dead leads | **Lead Follow-Up Pipeline + CRM automation** — every enquiry chased at the right time, in their voice | Pipeline moves without them touching it |
| Embarrassing website | **Telos Websites** — designed from scratch around their business, wired into the agents from day one | A site that finally matches the work |
| Night-time admin | **Booking, quote follow-up, invoice chasing, review requests** — the repetitive loops automated | Evenings back |
| No content, no time | **Telos Media** — posts, AI video, and ads run for them from ~£150/mo | Visible online without doing it themselves |
| Agency distrust | **Fully managed, flat monthly, no lock-in jargon** — we build, we run it, they own the results | A supplier that behaves like staff, not a vendor |

The one-line version: **we are the technology department they could never
afford to hire, customised to how they already work — from ~£100/month.**

## 4. How to reach them (channels + outreach angle)

**Channels, in priority order:**

1. **Local, in-person Bristol** — trade counters, business networking (BNI,
   chambers), gym/salon owners you already know. This ICP buys from people
   they've met. One good local case study compounds.
2. **Direct outreach with evidence** (the lead-finder + outreach agents are
   already built for this): find local businesses with weak/outdated sites,
   send a short personal note referencing *their* specific leak — e.g. "I
   called at 2pm Tuesday and nobody could take a booking."
3. **Referrals from adjacent trades** — accountants, bookkeepers, and trade
   suppliers who serve the same owners and are asked "do you know a website
   guy?" weekly.
4. **Meta + Google ads** (once policies/consent are live — they now are):
   geo-targeted to Bristol/SW, pointed at the waitlist and demo showcase.
5. **The demo showcase itself** — the pizzeria/salon/coffee phone demos are
   the sales pitch. Send the /websites link; let them scroll a business that
   looks like theirs.

**The outreach angle:** never sell "AI". Sell the missed call, the 9pm admin,
the embarrassing website. Lead with one specific, checkable observation about
their business, offer one specific fix with a number attached, and a
15-minute call. British, plain-spoken, zero jargon — exactly the voice the
site already uses.

## 5. Who it's NOT for (disqualifiers)

Stop the conversation early when you see:

- **No enquiry flow to save.** Pure walk-in retail, cash-only, or businesses
  with no bookings/quotes — our systems amplify demand handling; they can't
  create demand from zero.
- **E-commerce and product startups.** Different problem space (inventory,
  checkout, CAC); our stack and demos aren't built for it.
- **Committee buyers / corporates.** Procurement cycles, IT sign-off,
  security questionnaires — the £100–500/mo model can't carry that sales
  cost.
- **"Build me an app" founders.** People with an idea and no operating
  business. We customise technology *around* a working business.
- **DIY tinkerers.** Owners who want to operate the tools themselves and
  bargain on price — they churn; the value is in *fully managed*.
- **Can't sustain ~£100–150/mo.** If the retainer is a stretch, they'll
  churn in month three. Kindly point them at Squarespace and stay friendly.
- **Regulated-advice edge cases** — anything needing medical/legal/financial
  advice *generated by the AI* (receptionist booking a physio slot is fine;
  an agent giving clinical advice is not).

## 6. Messaging angles to reuse

Tested lines and frames for outreach, ads, and content:

1. **The missed call.** "A missed call doesn't cost you one job. It costs
   you every referral that client would have sent." (Already on the site —
   it's the strongest line we have.)
2. **The maths.** "Your receptionist would cost £22k a year. Ours starts at
   £100 a month and never takes lunch."
3. **The mirror.** "Your work is excellent. Your website says otherwise.
   One of those is easy to fix."
4. **The 9pm admin.** "You didn't start a business to do admin at 9pm.
   Every quote chased, every reminder sent, every review requested — done
   before you sit down."
5. **The anti-template.** "Most software makes you bend your business around
   it. We work the other way — we learn how you operate, then build the
   system that fits." (Mirrors the homepage statement — keep it consistent.)
6. **The anti-agency.** "No retainer mystery. You'll see the design before we
   write a line of code, and you approve everything before it goes live."
7. **Local proof.** "Built in Bristol, for businesses like yours — here's
   one you can scroll." (Link the phone-demo showcase.)
8. **The core line.** "Customising Technology for Your Business" — use it as
   the sign-off/strapline across outreach so every touch reinforces it.
